About Mrs. A

My name is Kristin Alarcon, and I have been cheering and dancing for over 18 years. I have been involved in cheer and dance as a participant, teacher, coach, and judge. Most recently, I serve as the Director of Media and Tabulation for Judges' Affiliation of the Oregon Dance and Drill Coaches' Association. For two sesons, I was a member of the South Sound Nightingales, a semi-pro cheer team for the South Sound Nighthawks football team. While living in Federal Way, I coached the varsity cheer team at Federal Way High School (where I was also the business teacher), and prior to that I coached the Eastmont High School dance team for three seasons. I have taught courses in hip-hop, jazz, tap, and contemporary for various dance studios in Oregon and Washington for several years. While attending the University of Oregon in 2004-2008, I served as the choreographer and captain of the UO Hip-Hop team, the Jam Squad. At Clackamas High School (Portland, OR), I was a member of the highly competitive varsity dance team for four years, and served as a captain my senior year as well as taking first place at the state drill down competition.


So Why Bows?

I have to admit, I sort of stumbled into this whole deal. I've been teaching Business for several years, and this year I started teaching Web Design at the high school level. I was brainstorming theoretical business ideas I could use as an example for the final business-web page project, and decided that I could pretend I sold the cheer bows I make for myself and my various teams. After putting together the example site, I showed it to my students and team members, and the amount of positive feedback was overwhelming! I'd always gotten compliments on the bows when I wear them, but putting the site together took the exposure to a whole other level. I decided to take my hobby and turn it into something totally worthwhile!
